What I focus on...

As a pharmacy student and trained biostatistician, I am enthusiastic about analysis & modeling in medication outcomes, harnessing quantitative methods and causal inference to investigate real-world evidence (RWE) in the context of pharm sci and public health.
Through drug development pipelines, I have expertise in these aspects:

  • Pharmacoepidemiology 药物流行病学: Evaluating drug safety (e.g., DDIs, polypharmacy & deprescribing) and policy-driven health disparities.
  • Pharmacometrics 定量药理学: Applying statistical & mechanistic PK modeling to assess therapy outcomes and individualize treatment strategies.
  • Real World Data 真实世界数据: Analyzing large-scale claims, EHRs, PROs, and surveys for actionable insights into pharmacy delivery and population health.

Doxorubicin & Trastuzumab Cardiotoxicity QSP-PBPK-TD Platform

22022.04–2023.08

Research Assistant. Supervised by Dr Hua He, Center of Drug Metabolism and Pharmacokinetics, School of Pharmacy, China Pharmaceutical University.

  • Developed an in silico platform integrating QSP-PBPK modeling and hiPSC-CM data to predict cardiotoxicity risk of anti-tumor drug combinations, conducting simulations and sensitivity analyses to evaluate effects of doxorubicin & trastuzumab on cardiac functions.
  • Ensured reliability and clinical relevance by validating in silico predictions against clinical trial data and adjusting parameters to reflect physiological differences.
  • Gained insights into factors driving cardiotoxicity in anti-tumor therapies, identified synergistic effects of doxorubicin and trastuzumab, and highlighted individual patient variability in drug response.
  • Provided valuable feedback by offering a predictive tool for safer drug combination therapies and informing clinical guidelines to reduce cardiotoxicity incidence, thus improving patient outcomes.
